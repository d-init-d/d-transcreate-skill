# Scene 3 — The Price of Seeing

By the third night, Seren understood the pattern. Each time she lit the lantern, the hidden paths grew clearer — but so did the presence of the Hollow. They circled the camp now, visible only at the edge of her vision: tall figures made of absence, like holes cut in the shape of people.

"You need to stop using it," Moth said. The spirit's glow had dimmed to a nervous flicker. "Every time you look at what's hidden, they get closer. That's the bargain Kael Morrow made — sight for proximity. Knowledge for vulnerability."

Seren stared at the lantern on her desk. In its glass panels, the etched map symbols seemed to move, rearranging themselves into new configurations. She had already copied three hidden paths onto her master chart. Three paths that could reshape how people moved through the marshlands. Three paths that were worth more than gold to the right buyer.

"One more," she said. "There's a path marked in the deep marsh — it leads somewhere important. I can feel it."

"Seren." Moth's voice was sharp, parental. "The last cartographer who used that lantern was Kael Morrow himself. Do you know what happened to him?"

"He disappeared."

"He didn't disappear. The Hollow *took* him. He saw too much, and they folded him into the blank spaces between maps — the places that exist but cannot be drawn."

Seren's hand hovered over the lantern's dial. Outside, the hollow sound came again, closer now. She could feel it in her chest, a vibration like a plucked string.

She turned the dial. Light bloomed across the map one final time.

"Then I'd better draw fast," she said.
