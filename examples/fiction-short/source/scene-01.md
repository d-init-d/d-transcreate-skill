# Scene 1 — The Ruined Tower

The tower stood at the edge of the Ashveil Marshes, half-swallowed by fog and creeping moss. Seren had marked it on her map three days ago — a faint circle with a question mark, the cartographer's shorthand for *unverified structure*. Now, standing before its crumbling archway, she wished she had left it as a question mark forever.

"You're stalling," said Moth, perched on her satchel strap. The little spirit resembled a paper lantern with wings, glowing faintly amber in the grey morning light. "The tide-fog rolls in by midday. If you want what's inside, move now."

Seren adjusted her compass and stepped through the arch. Inside, the tower was larger than its exterior suggested — a common trick of old Kaelish architecture, where builders folded space the way a tailor folds cloth. Shelves lined the curved walls, empty except for dust and the skeletal remains of books long since rotted.

At the centre of the room, resting on a stone pedestal, sat a lantern. It was made of dark iron and pale glass, no larger than her fist. The glass panels were etched with tiny map symbols — contour lines, river marks, compass roses — so fine they might have been drawn by a needle.

"The Lantern of Kael Morrow," Moth whispered, wings flickering. "I didn't think it was real."

Seren reached for it. The moment her fingers touched the cold iron handle, every map in her satchel began to glow.
