# Scene 2 — The Hidden Paths

Back at camp, Seren spread her largest map across the folding table — the one she had spent two seasons surveying, covering the entire Ashveil region from the Salt Cliffs to the Thornwood border. She set the lantern beside it and turned the small brass dial on its base.

Light spilled across the parchment, warm and golden. And then the map changed.

New lines appeared where none had been before. Paths wound through terrain she had marked as impassable. A bridge crossed the Sorrow River at a point where no bridge should exist. Deep in the Thornwood, a settlement appeared — tiny squares for buildings, a circle for a well — in a valley her surveys had recorded as empty wilderness.

"Hidden paths," Seren breathed. "Paths that someone erased from every known map."

Moth landed on the table's edge, tilting to study the glowing lines. "Not erased. *Sealed.* The old cartographers didn't just draw the world — they decided what the world was allowed to show. Kael Morrow disagreed with their choices."

Seren traced the new bridge with her fingertip. The line felt warm, almost alive. "If these paths are real, they change everything. Trade routes, borders, the entire political geography of the marshlands."

"They're real," Moth said quietly. "And that's precisely why they were hidden. Some paths are kept secret because the things that walk them prefer not to be found."

As if in answer, the lantern's light flickered. Somewhere in the fog beyond the camp, Seren heard a sound like wind passing through an empty doorway — a hollow, resonant note that made her teeth ache.

The Hollow were listening.
