# Style Sheet

## Voice

### Register
Formal legal/regulatory register. Language must be precise, unambiguous, and authoritative.

### Formality
High formality throughout. No contractions, colloquialisms, or informal phrasing. Use complete verb forms and standard legal constructions.

### Sentence Rhythm
Mirror source sentence structure closely. Legal documents require precision over fluency; do not break complex sentences into shorter ones unless the Vietnamese rendering would be genuinely ambiguous.

### Genre Constraints
Maintain the declarative, impersonal tone of corporate legal documents. Avoid conversational softeners. Preserve conditional and obligatory modality (shall, may, must) with appropriate Vietnamese equivalents.

### Notes
Legal/policy texts prioritize precision and enforceability over readability. The Vietnamese rendering must be legally equivalent in meaning to the English source. Vietnamese data protection legislation (Nghị định 13/2023/NĐ-CP) provides reference for standard legal phrasing in this domain.

---

## Language Conventions

### Dialogue Punctuation
N/A — legal documents do not contain dialogue.

### Titles and Headings
Preserve original numbering (§1, §2, etc.). Translate heading text. Use sentence case for section titles consistent with Vietnamese legal document conventions.

### Names
Keep company name "NovaTech Solutions" and "NovaTech" untranslated. Keep email addresses and URLs unchanged.

### Terms of Address
Use impersonal constructions. Refer to the user as "bạn" (second person, neutral-formal) consistent with Vietnamese software terms of service conventions. Refer to the company as "chúng tôi" (we, formal).

### Honorifics
N/A — no honorifics in source text.

### Number, Date, and Unit Conventions
- Dates: retain "1 January 2025" format as "ngày 1 tháng 1 năm 2025" (Vietnamese legal date format).
- Numbers in parenthetical clarifications: retain digits with Vietnamese unit, e.g., "thirty (30) calendar days" → "ba mươi (30) ngày dương lịch".
- Durations: spell out number followed by digit in parentheses, matching source pattern.

### Citation Conventions
N/A — no academic citations in source.

### Footnote and Translator-Note Policy
No translator notes. Legal documents must not contain editorial additions. If a term requires clarification, use parenthetical gloss on first occurrence only (e.g., "Cán bộ Bảo vệ Dữ liệu (Data Protection Officer)").

### Notes
Vietnamese legal documents follow a formal register similar to legislative drafting. The pronoun pair "chúng tôi / bạn" is standard in Vietnamese terms of service and privacy policies for technology platforms, balancing formality with accessibility.

## Adaptation Rules

### Idioms
N/A — legal text does not contain idiomatic expressions. If any appear, render the literal legal meaning.

### Humor
N/A — not applicable to legal documents.

### Cultural References
N/A — no cultural references in source.

### Metaphors
N/A — legal text avoids figurative language. Translate all language literally.

### Repetition
Preserve deliberate repetition of defined terms. Legal documents intentionally repeat exact terminology for precision; do not vary for stylistic reasons.

### Songs, Poems, and Quoted Material
N/A.

### Notes
Legal translation requires strict fidelity. No adaptation, localization of meaning, or creative interpretation is permitted. Every clause must carry the same legal force in the target language.

## Forbidden Patterns

| Pattern | Reason |
|---------|--------|
| Informal pronouns (mày, tao, cậu) | Inappropriate register for legal documents |
| Varying terminology for defined terms | Legal precision requires consistent term usage throughout |
| Adding explanatory content not in source | Alters legal scope and meaning of the document |
| Omitting conditional/obligatory modality | Changes legal force (e.g., "shall" vs "may" distinction must be preserved) |
| Using loan words when approved Vietnamese equivalent exists | Glossary mandates Vietnamese terms for legal concepts |
| Splitting or merging numbered sections | Alters document structure and cross-reference integrity |

### Notes
Forbidden patterns are designed to prevent common errors in legal translation that could alter the enforceability or interpretation of the document. The glossary takes precedence for all terminology decisions.
